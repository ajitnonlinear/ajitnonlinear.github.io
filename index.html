About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Division of Dynamics, Department of Mechanical Engineering at Lodz University of Technology, Poland. My present research focuses on machine learning for complex dynamical systems, with particular emphasis on extreme events, synchronization phenomena, partial observability, and reservoir computing. I work on predicting critical transitions such as cluster and global synchronization in networks of nonlinear oscillators using data-driven approaches validated on both numerical and experimental datasets.

Previously, as a Postdoctoral Fellow at IISER Pune, I developed reservoir computing–based frameworks for predicting chaotic dynamical systems under partial observation, demonstrating accurate short-term prediction and faithful reconstruction of long-term attractor dynamics. My Ph.D. research at NIT Sikkim focused on nonlinear time-series analysis and modeling of financial markets, where I investigated multiscale time horizons, structural breaks, and extreme events such as market crashes using tools from statistical physics. Broadly, my research interests span data-driven dynamical systems, extreme-event prediction, physics-informed machine learning, and emerging directions in quantum-enhanced learning for complex systems.
