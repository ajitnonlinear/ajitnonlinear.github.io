About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Division of Dynamics, Department of Mechanical Engineering at Lodz University of Technology, Poland. My research lies at the intersection of machine learning and complex dynamical systems, with a focus on understanding, modeling, and predicting nonlinear and chaotic phenomena from limited and partial observations. A central theme of my work is the study of extreme events, where rare but high-impact transitions challenge conventional modeling approaches.

I develop data-driven and physics-inspired machine learning frameworks, particularly reservoir computing and recurrent architectures, to extract dynamical structure from time-series data and anticipate critical transitions. My research spans coupled oscillators, networked dynamical systems, and financial markets, emphasizing multiscale dynamics, synchronization, and collective behavior. Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Fellow at IISER Pune under Prof. Amit Apte and a Research Associate-II under Dr. Pranay Goel. I completed my Ph.D. from NIT Sikkim, and my broader interests include data-driven dynamical systems, extreme-event prediction, partial observability, physics-informed machine learning, and emerging directions in quantum-enhanced learning for complex systems.
